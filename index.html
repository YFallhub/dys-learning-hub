📞 Crucial Conversation with School - This Week

Parent script for principal/teacher:
"My child has a medical diagnosis of dysgraphia. We request immediate authorization to use a computer and reduce handwriting requirements. This is a medically necessary accommodation, not a preference."

Specifically request:
• Permission to bring laptop to class
• Permission to answer with keywords instead of complete sentences
• Extended time for tests
• Copy of class notes (to avoid handwritten note-taking)

🏠 Changes at Home - Today

1. No-writing homework zone: Computer/tablet required for all homework
2. "Keywords" rule: Never write complete sentences for homework
3. 10-minute timer: Daily typing practice on TypingClub
4. Voice recorder/app: To "write" by speaking

💪 Encouragement message: "You don't have to suffer with writing anymore. We're going to find other ways to show how smart you are!"

🎮 Motivating Games to Try Right Now

For typing: Start with NitroType.com - Car racing by typing!
For concentration: Forest app - Plant virtual trees
For organization: Gamify your homework with Todoist and its badges

✍️ Writing Solutions

×

🎤 Voice-to-Text (Primary Method)

Google Docs Voice Typing: Tools → Voice typing. Speak naturally, say punctuation out loud.

Tips: Speak in complete sentences • Say "period" "comma" "new paragraph" • Review and edit after dictating

⌨️ Typing (Secondary Method)

Goal: 30-40 words per minute by end of semester

Practice: 10 minutes daily on TypingClub or NitroType

Why it matters: Once you type fast enough, you can do ALL homework on computer

🔑 Keyword Strategy (Accommodation)

Instead of: "Napoleon Bonaparte was the Emperor of France from 1804 to 1815 and led many military campaigns."

Write: "Napoleon = French Emperor 1804-1815, military leader"

✓ Shows you know the material
✓ Saves enormous energy
✓ Must be approved as accommodation by teacher

🤖 AI Writing Assistants

Grammarly↗ - Fixes spelling, grammar, suggests better words

Hemingway Editor↗ - Makes writing clear and simple

Note: These are editing tools, not for generating content. Use them to improve YOUR writing.

🎯 Talk to your teacher: "Due to my dysgraphia diagnosis, may I submit typed work or use keywords instead of full sentences? This accommodation allows me to demonstrate my knowledge without the barrier of handwriting."
📱 For Younger Students (Ages 7-12)

Start with: Voice typing + TypingClub practice + Keywords for short answers

📝 For Older Students (Ages 13+)

Master: Touch typing 40+ wpm + Professional dictation + Advanced tools like Grammarly

⚡ Executive Function

×

🧠 What is Executive Function?

The mental skills that help you: start tasks, stay focused, remember instructions, manage time, and switch between activities. Many kids with dysgraphia/dyspraxia also have executive function challenges.

⏰ Time Management Strategies

Pomodoro Technique: Work 25 minutes → 5 minute break → Repeat

Visual timers: Time Timer app↗ shows time "disappearing"

Backward planning: Start with due date, work backward to figure out when to start

📋 Breaking Down Big Tasks

Instead of: "Write book report" (too big, causes paralysis)

Break into:
1. Read chapters 1-3 (30 min)
2. Write 3 key points about characters (10 min)
3. Dictate intro paragraph (5 min)
4. Dictate body paragraphs (15 min)
5. Write conclusion (5 min)
6. Review and edit (10 min)

🎯 Focus Tools

Forest↗ - Plant virtual tree, don't use phone or tree dies

Focus Keeper↗ - Pomodoro timer with statistics

Freedom↗ - Block distracting websites during homework time

🗂️ Organization Systems

Physical space: Designate ONE spot for backpack, laptop, chargers ("launch pad")

Digital space: Folder system: School → [Subject] → [Type: Notes, Homework, Tests]

Daily routine: Same time, same place for homework every day = less mental energy

🎲 Gamification Strategies

Turn executive function into a game:

• Todoist karma points for completing tasks
• Forest app growing virtual forest
• Personal challenge: "Can I start homework within 5 minutes today?"
• Reward system: Points toward chosen activity/treat

💡 Key insight: You're not lazy! Your brain needs external systems (apps, timers, alarms, lists) to do what others do internally. That's completely normal for dysgraphia/dyspraxia/ADHD.

🏆 Building a Daily Routine

5-minute warm-up: NitroType (typing) or Forest (focus check)

Homework session: Use Pomodoro timer (25 min work / 5 min break)

Evening wind-down: 5 minutes brain training (Lumosity/Peak)

Weekend: Longer typing practice (15-20 min) or Habitica quest

🎯 Parent tip: These games replace the need for traditional "handwriting practice" which is often frustrating and ineffective for dysgraphia. Focus energy on skills that will actually help (typing, organization, focus).
