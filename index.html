üè• Professionals to Know

Occupational Therapist (OT): Specialist who evaluates, rehabilitates and compensates for disorders like dysgraphia. Prescribes adaptive equipment and works on hand-eye coordination.

üí∞ Often not fully covered by insurance - Expect $80-150/session
Physical Therapist: Works on gross motor skills, balance, coordination and psychomotor development of children with dyspraxia.
üí∞ Usually partially covered by insurance
Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP): Works on language and learning issues that may accompany dysgraphia.
‚úÖ Often covered by insurance with referral
Neuropsychologist: Conducts psychometric tests measuring memory, attention, language and brain functions.
üí∞ Usually not covered by insurance - $1500-3000 for full evaluation

üèõÔ∏è IEP/504 - Your Key Partner

What is an IEP? Individualized Education Program - A legal document that describes the special education instruction, supports, and services a child with disabilities needs to thrive in school.

What is a 504 Plan? Section 504 Plan - Provides accommodations to ensure students with disabilities have equal access to education.

Main missions:
‚Ä¢ Assess compensation needs and develop a personalized plan
‚Ä¢ Recognize disability and make decisions adapted to each child's needs
‚Ä¢ Create a personalized compensation plan based on the child's goals

üìç Important: Work with your school district to set up appropriate accommodations. It's your child's legal right!

üéí School Support Options

504 Plan: For students with disabilities who need accommodations but not special education services.
‚úÖ No special education classification needed
‚úÖ Valid through high school
‚úÖ Provides classroom accommodations

IEP (Individualized Education Program): For students ages 3-21 with disabilities requiring special education.
‚úÖ Can provide adaptive equipment (computer)
‚úÖ Can include aide support
‚úÖ Can modify curriculum and testing

Accommodations vs Modifications:
‚Ä¢ Accommodations change HOW a student learns (extended time, assistive tech)
‚Ä¢ Modifications change WHAT a student learns (reduced assignments, different standards)

üë©‚Äçüè´ Educational Aide - School Support

Paraprofessionals or educational aides provide support to students with disabilities under the supervision of teachers.

How to get an aide:
‚Ä¢ Request through IEP team meeting

‚Ä¢ Specified in IEP document

‚ö†Ô∏è Note: Not all students with dysgraphia need a full-time aide. Many succeed with accommodations alone.
